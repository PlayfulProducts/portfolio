Educational Games

2015 - 2020

Board games, card games & paper activities • Young Researchers magazine • ages 8-12

Designed as pull-out supplements and standalone inserts for the Young Researchers magazine, these educational games turned each issue's scientific theme into something children could play with, share, and return to. From board games to card games, dice mechanics, and paper-based puzzles, every piece was designed to make learning feel like play.

Game Design Meets Graphic Design

Each game started from the editorial content: what is this issue teaching, and how can a game reinforce that in a playful way. The design had to carry both jobs at once, looking inviting and fun while communicating clearly.

What Was Made

Games across the series included illustrated board games following scientific narratives, card matching and sorting games built around taxonomy or classification themes, dice-and-movement games with branching outcomes, question-and-answer card decks, and paper crafts with a game component. Each was designed to be played with minimal preparation.

Illustration & Visual Language

All game artwork was original, drawn to match the visual language of the Young Researchers magazine while leaning into a slightly looser, more playful register. Characters were expressive and themed per issue. Boards used colour gradients and illustrated zones to guide movement without heavy text instruction. The overall look was warm, energetic, and built to be used. These were pages meant to be touched, moved across, and worn out with use.

Production

Games were produced print-ready as part of each issue's press-ready package, designed to print on standard magazine paper stock and function without specialist materials. Digital versions were also prepared for download and home printing through the magazine's subscriber platform.
